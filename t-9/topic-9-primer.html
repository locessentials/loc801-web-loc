TOPIC 9

Localization Quality Assurance

Building a multilingual site is only half the job. This topic is about making sure it actually works across languages, layouts, and user experiences. You'll learn a structured approach to LQA, practice it on a real site, and hear from a developer about what good localization looks like from the engineering side.

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Apply a structured LQA checklist to evaluate multilingual websites across five categories: translated content, code integrity, layout, accessibility, and discoverability
  • Identify and document localization issues clearly and with supporting evidence
  • Analyze how localization errors affect site effectiveness, credibility, and reach
  • Understand localization from a developer's perspective, including common engineering oversights and best practices for building multilingual products
  • Conduct an independent LQA review of a live multilingual site and reflect on your findings
