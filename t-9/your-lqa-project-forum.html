TOPIC 9

Your LQA Project

Now that you've practiced with a guided example, it's time to run your own LQA review on a live multilingual site and share what you find.

🧭 Forum Overview

The Speedrun LQA Project focused on one specific category: completeness. For this project, you'll choose another category from the LQA checklist, such as layout, accessibility, or discoverability, and apply it to a bilingual or multilingual website of your choosing. The goal is to keep the scope manageable while still doing a careful, methodical review.

After your review, you'll share your findings and reflect on what the issues you found mean in practice.

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Apply a focused LQA review to a real, live multilingual website
  • Document localization issues clearly and with supporting evidence
  • Analyze how localization errors affect a site's effectiveness for its users

📋 Instructions

1

Choose a Site

Pick any live website that has content in at least two languages. It can be a company site, a government portal, a nonprofit, a news outlet, anything publicly accessible. Avoid sites you've already reviewed in this course.

2

Choose One LQA Category

Select one of the five categories from the LQA checklist: translated content, code integrity, layout, accessibility, or discoverability. Apply that category's checklist items systematically across several pages of the site. Don't try to cover everything. A focused review of one category done well is more valuable than a rushed scan of all five.

3

Document Your Findings

Note the specific issues you found, where on the site you found them, and what checklist items they correspond to. Screenshots or direct quotes are helpful as evidence.

4

Post Your Reflection

Write a post for the forum that includes the URL of the site you reviewed, the LQA category you focused on, and your reflections on on the following:

  • What issues did you find? Were any of them surprising?
  • How do those issues affect the site's effectiveness? For example, do they risk misleading users, damaging credibility, or hurting discoverability?
  • Anything else you noticed or want to share about your experience doing the review?
5

Engage with peers

If you're working on this activity for a class: Read at least one other post and reply with a substantive comment. Did their findings match patterns you've seen? Do you agree with their analysis of the impact? Prioritize posts with fewer replies.

For independent learners: Share your findings with a colleague or mentor and discuss what you both would prioritize fixing first and why.

📝 Discussion Prompt

What did your LQA review reveal?

In your forum post, include the URL of the site you reviewed and the LQA category you focused on. Then describe the issues you found and reflect on what they mean. Were any findings unexpected? How do the issues you identified affect the experience of people using the site, or the site's ability to reach them in the first place? Feel free to share anything else you took away from doing the review.
