TOPIC 8

Example CMS Quote

A sample localization quote for a WordPress site moving from Spanish to English. Use this as a reference when scoping your own project and evaluating the quotes you receive.

📄 How to Use This Example

The quote below is a realistic example of what a localization vendor might submit for a WordPress site. It covers the full scope of a small-to-mid-size project: hosting setup, plugin licensing, localized SEO analysis, engineering, writing (including translation and transcreation), CAT tool-assisted work on shorter strings, and sensitivity reading.

The annotations throughout explain what each line item means, why it appears in the quote, and what to watch for when you receive something similar from a real vendor. As you compare quotes for your own project, ask yourself whether the same categories are represented and whether the pricing and rationale hold up.

Course Note

This is a sample quote created for Loc801. The vendor, client, and pricing are fictional but realistic. Real quotes vary significantly by vendor, market, and scope.

Notice a few things here: CAT tools and language switching are not mentioned because the intention is to make a separate website specifically for a U.S. audence. The SEO metadata is treated as a separate service from writing because it requires different skills and research. Sensitivity readings with specialist reviewers appear as their own line items, as part of the quality workflow. Project management is calculated at about 20% of the total cost of the rest of the services. And the long-term section makes ongoing costs visible upfront rather than leaving them as a surprise after the project ends.

When you evaluate quotes from real vendors, check whether these categories are present. Their absence may not be a red flag on its own, but it is always worth asking about.
