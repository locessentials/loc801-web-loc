TOPIC 10

Conclusions & Project Showcase

You've built real skills across nine topics. Now it's time to look back at what you've learned, pick up a few visual design fundamentals, and publish a bilingual portfolio that showcases your work to clients, colleagues, and future employers.

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Reflect on the key concepts, frameworks, and skills developed across the full course
  • Recognize the connections between translation quality, technical skills, discoverability, and professional collaboration
  • Apply basic visual design principles like layout, typography, color, and accessibility to a professional web project
  • Build and publish a bilingual portfolio website that presents your localization projects to a professional audience
  • Identify directions for continued learning beyond this course

📋 Action Items

Lecture
Course Review

Look back at the full arc of Loc801: the four phases of the course, the major takeaways, the projects you completed, and where to go from here.

Lecture
Visual Design

Learn the design fundamentals you need to present your work professionally: layout, typography, color accessibility, and culturally inclusive imagery choices.

Project
Portfolio Project

Fork a bilingual portfolio template, personalize it with your own projects and content, and publish a live site on GitHub Pages that you can share with clients, employers, and colleagues.

⏱️ Estimated Time
Plan for approximately 6–8 hours to complete all activities in this topic. The Portfolio Project is the centerpiece. It's your opportunity to bring everything together and publish a professional, bilingual showcase of the work you've done throughout this course.
