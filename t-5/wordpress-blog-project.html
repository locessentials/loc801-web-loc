WordPress Blog Project

Create a WordPress blog post that demonstrates your understanding of traditional SEO, keyword research, and LLM discoverability principles.

üéØ Project Overview

In this project, you'll create a WordPress site and write a blog post optimized for both traditional search engines and large language models. This hands-on assignment ties together everything you've learned about SEO, keyword research, and content discoverability.

The goal is to write bespoke web content in either U.S. English or Mexican Spanish, depending on your specific audience and project goals. Remember: websites designed for a specific region and audience are often more effective than bilingual sites with language pickers and uneven coverage across languages.

Why WordPress?

WordPress powers approximately 43% of all websites globally, making it the most widely used content management system in the world. While Wix is also popular in Mexico, WordPress's dominance in the market means that it's valuable to understand how to use it for anyone working in web localization. The platform's flexibility, extensive plugin ecosystem, and built-in SEO features make it an great choice for learning how to optimize content for discoverability.

Install the Yoast SEO Plugin

Navigate to the Plugins section in your WordPress dashboard and search for "Yoast SEO." Install and activate the free version of the plugin. Yoast will add an SEO analysis panel at the bottom of each page and blog post you create.

The Yoast plugin provides real-time feedback on several key SEO factors:

  • Focus keyphrase usage - How well you've incorporated your target keyword
  • Meta description - The summary that appears in search results
  • Image optimization - Whether you've included images with alt text
  • Internal and external links - Links to other pages and authoritative sources
  • Readability - Text complexity and structure (focus less on this‚Äîyour writing style may not conform to Yoast's readability priorities, and that's okay. SEO is about producing high-quality content, not necessarily following rigid readability formulas)

‚úçÔ∏è Part 2: Write Your Blog Post

Now comes the main part of the assignment: writing a blog post that demonstrates your understanding of SEO and LLM discoverability.

1

Choose Your Topic and Audience

Select a topic you're knowledgeable about or interested in exploring. Critically, you need to identify a specific audience for this post. Don't just say "Mexico" or "United States"‚Äîthese are countries, not audiences. Instead, think about who exactly would benefit from reading this content.

Examples of Specific Audiences

  • Undergraduate translation students in Mexico
  • Small business owners in Texas looking to expand to Mexican markets
  • Spanish-English interpreters preparing for medical certification
  • High school teachers in California teaching Spanish as a second language
  • First-generation college students at the University of Florida
2

Conduct Keyword Research

Use a keyword research tool like KWFinder (which offers a free trial) to identify a focus keyword for your post. Consider:

  • Search volume - How many people are searching for this term?
  • Keyword difficulty - How competitive is this keyword?
  • Search intent - What are people actually looking for when they search this term?
  • Relevance to your specific audience

Take screenshots of your research‚Äîyou'll need to include at least one in your reflection.

3

Write for Traditional SEO

Draft your blog post with traditional SEO best practices in mind:

  • Include your focus keyword naturally in the title, first paragraph, and throughout the content
  • Write a compelling meta description (the summary that appears in search results)
  • Use headings (H2, H3) to structure your content
  • Add at least one image with descriptive alt text
  • Include at least external links (to authoritative sources)
  • Aim for comprehensive coverage of your topic

Use the Yoast SEO panel to guide your optimization. Your goal is to get a green "happy face" for the SEO score. Take a screenshot when you achieve this‚Äîyou'll need it for your reflection.

4

Write for LLM Discoverability

Beyond traditional SEO, optimize your content for large language models:

  • Use clear, direct language that answers specific questions
  • Include relevant context and definitions
  • Structure information logically with clear sections
  • Provide authoritative information with proper attribution
  • Use structured data where appropriate (lists, tables, step-by-step instructions)
  • Write content that would be valuable if quoted directly in an AI response
5

Publish Your Post

Once you're satisfied with your content and have achieved a good SEO score in Yoast, publish your blog post. Make sure it's publicly accessible.

üìß Newsletter Strategy for Content Distribution

Creating great blog content is only half the battle‚Äîyou also need to ensure your audience actually sees it. This is where newsletter strategy comes in.

An email newsletter provides a direct channel to your audience that you control. Unlike social media, where algorithms determine whether people see your content, email newsletters guarantee delivery to subscribers' inboxes. This makes them an invaluable tool for content creators, businesses, and anyone building an audience online.

How Newsletter Strategy Works with Blog Content

The typical workflow looks like this:


  1. Write multiple blog posts over time, each optimized for SEO and LLM discoverability
  2. Create a newsletter (using platforms like Substack, Mailchimp, or ConvertKit) that goes out weekly, biweekly, or monthly
  3. Include blurbs in your newsletter that summarize each blog post with a compelling hook
  4. Link to your website where readers can access the full article

Why This Strategy Works

The newsletter includes just enough information to spark interest, but readers must visit your website to read the complete article. This drives traffic to your site, improves your SEO metrics (like time on page and bounce rate), and builds a habit of your audience returning to your website. Plus, you're not dependent on social media algorithms showing your content‚Äîyou have a direct relationship with your readers.


Many successful bloggers and content creators use this dual approach: SEO-optimized blog posts for discoverability, combined with email newsletters for guaranteed delivery and audience retention. Consider how you might integrate this strategy into your own content creation workflow.
